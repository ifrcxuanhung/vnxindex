IFRC Index presents new websites dedicated to Vietnam Indexes especially

These websites offer a wealth of information, documentation, data and tools related to Vietnamese indices calculated by local (HOSE, HNX Stock Exchange, PVN, IFRC, ...)  and international (Market Vector, FTSE, MSCI, STOXX Limited, DAXPLUS, THOMSON REUTERS, S&P, ...) suppliers.

IFRC introduces a complete family of 280 representative indices, including Benchmark, Size, Sectors, Blue Chips, Strategy and Theme indexes, calculated in Price and Total return versions and converted into 4 different major currencies: VND, USD, EUR and JPY. They are historically reconstructed since 2008.

Other indexing innovations are presented in the IFRC Lab indexes section.

IFRC Cooperations and Projects

Intelligent Financial Research & Consulting (IFRC) is pleased to announce several cooperations with financial institutions and universities:

1- PSI and IFRC joins forces to create the first Indexes Provider Services in Vietnam

The Petrovietnam Securities Incorporated (PSI) and Intelligent Financial Research & Consulting Viet Nam (IFRC Viet Nam) have signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to pursue a continuing strategic cooperation regarding the potential development of index products and services in Viet Nam.

2- ICN Business School Group and IFRC are pleased to announce a new partnership to develop a new Women CEO indexes series

for Euro zone and Europe after the successful launching of the Viet Nam Women Indexes Series in early 2013

3- NEOMA Business School Group (Reims and Rouen French Business Schools) and IFRC are pleased to announce a new partnership to develop a sentiment index for the Real Estate in France

4- Vietnam Financial Database (www.vnfdb.com) is an ambitious Academic Cooperative Project

VNFDB is an academic cooperative project in order to centralize in a single point of access to different sources and types of data and documentation useful for research in Finance. The information mainly Vietnam and Asian regions. Comparative data from other countries and regions are also available.

Empirical research concerning financial markets is currently hindered by several factors, among others the high access costs to financial and stock exchange data and the difficulty to obtain quality data. That is why the VNFDB works to create verified, controlled and homogeneous databases over long periods, the same way CRSP does with American data.

The database covers different areas: economics, commodities, currencies, interest rates, bonds, equities, indexes, mutual funds, ... for vietnamese and international markets.
